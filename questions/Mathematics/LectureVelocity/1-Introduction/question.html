In Calculus we are often interested in computing how fast a quantity is changing. We may be interested in the average rate of change of a function on a given interval, or the instantaneous rate of change of a function at a specified point. For example, if the function represents the position of a moving object, the instantaneous rate of change is the velocity, and can be expressed in units of feet per second, or kilometers per hour. For a moving object with position function $s(t)$, the average velocity on the interval $t=t_i$ and $t=t_f$ is given by:

$$v_a = \frac{s(t_f) - s(t_i)}{t_f - t_i}$$

Without loss of generality, we can write the average rate of change of a function $f(x)$ in the interval $[a, a+h]$ as:

$$v_a = \frac{f(a+h) - f(x)}{h}$$
